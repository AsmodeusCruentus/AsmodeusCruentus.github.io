Cradle of Fear


Cradle of Fear is a British horror film, Directed by Alex Chandon.
Alex Changdon took great inspiration from the Anthology films that were
produced by Amicus Productions around the 70s.
The Amicus Production produced films like
"From beyond the grave"
"Tales from the Crypt"
"Asylum"
The Films runtime is around 120 minutes and consists of three 30 minute stories
that are then tied together by a fourth overall story.


The plot of the movie focuses around an serial killer named Kemper.
After being improsed in an insane asylum he starts causing mayhem for those at fault for his imprisonment.
Kemper was the son of an infamous satanist and used hypnosis to carry out his crimes.
The main character is "The Man" who is protrayed by Dani Filth who finds the perputrators of Kempers capture
and finds ways for them to get into accidents and die, all of which sparks the interested of Inspector Peter Neilson.
All these events remind Neilson of a serial killer he has imprisoned years ago.


The film didn't really catch the greatest of attention from the Horror fanatics or the Horror press due to its low budget filming,
It has however maintained viewership from fans of the band "Cradle of Filth" Since the bands frontman
Dani Filth portayes "The Man", even though the entire band appears in cameos in the movie.
This has caused fans of the band to watch the movie and even cosplay "The Man".


Alex Chandons partnership with the band started when he recorded their promo video for the song "From the Cradle to Enslave"
He later went on to assist in recording portions of other videos such as "Her Ghost in the Fog" & ""No Time to Cry
